On the road at the end of the world, MARCH 2022 - APRIL 2020



In the midst of coronavirus, I rented a car and jumped on the road. From the American Southwest to the coast of California and back. From big cities to podunk towns. I experienced the quintessential American road trip, all while the world was in the throws of a pandemic. These are the portraits and stories of the people who I encountered along the way.

(Hover over to reveal a short story)

Rustic Iron BBQ
Terlingua, TX


Don posed next to his piles of chopped wood used for smoking. he explains to us that business has been good since people have been escaping to the desert to avoid being locked inside. He plans to open a sit-down restaurant within the next year.

St. Elena Canyon
Big Bend National Park, TX


An elderly couple came down in their RV from Boulder, CO to visit a relative. They stopped into Big Bend to get some miles in on their bicycles. They both explain how they’ve never seen the world come to such a halt, especially having lived through WWII and the conflicts that followed. The woman tells me of the post-apocalyptic film On the Beach, and likens our current situation to it. They are both in their mid-80’s.

The Get Go
Marfa, TX


Pictured is the floor manager at the Get Go Grocery. A small independent shop, with a staff of 4 individuals. She recalls the day that people in town began to panic purchase. And explains to me that when there are 5 customers in the store at the same time, that to them that is what they consider busy.

The Big Texan
Amarillo, TX


Both employees had been working at the restaurant for several years. Their staff went from 300 down to 40 in a single work meeting. As we were chatting, furloughed employees walked in and out of the building carrying large plastic bags. They were filled with non-perishables, sanitary supplies, and other miscellaneous items that the company had bought to give to their former employees to help them.

Gig Driver
Downtown Santa Fe, NM


A local Uber driver waiting in the early morning. He tells me he’s averaging about 2 rides a day. He seems indifferent to the situation. Suddenly in the middle of our conversation, he motioned up to the sky, “it’s up to Allah” he said. We finish our conversation, and as I make my way back to the car, he slowly rolls up besides me and bids me farewell, and wished me good luck on my journey.

The Butterfly Inn
Coconino National Forest, AZ


The Butterfly Inn recently went under new management. The woman pictured had been brought in to breathe new life into the establishment. Originally from Phoenix, she moved just outside the forest with her 4 year old daughter. Since COVID-19 took off, she noticed a decline in lodging, but activity in the forest remains the same. It begins to snow, so we moved quickly to take the photo. Her daughter bursts out onto the porch beside us with her dog and play as the snowfall became heavier.

Cat & Cloud Coffee
Santa Cruz, CA


Cat and Cloud was a place I’d been wanting to visit for several years. I finally got the chance to swing by the original shop on my trip and was greeted with a damn fine cup of coffee with some great conversation to match. We discussed virtual tip jar conundrums, service industry holes and woes, and why Zoccolis Deli is the best in town. Delicious.

Professor
Del Monte Beach Monterrey, CA


After and early morning walk on the beach I returned to me car to wash the sand of my feet. An older woman walks past and gives me a big smile, she notices my license plate isn’t from California and asks me what brought me to Monterey. At the time, Gov. Cuomo had become the icon for leadership, praised for his controlled and honest demeanor. We chatted about our mutual admiration for him and several other state and local politicians that have stepped up to the plate in lieu of the president. She tipped me off about a hidden coastal trail (pictured in the book) off of Ribera Dr. It was every bit as beautiful as she described.

Friends
South Lake Tahoe, CA


4 friends returning back to their car from the lake with their dogs. We begin to chat. Austin, I tell them, is a seasonal town revolving around events like ACL and SXSW. Similarly Lake Tahoe depends on heavy snowfall for business. This past winter had been particularly bad, low amounts of snowfall combined with COVID-19 killed tourism in the alpine town. One guy mentions “Miracle March,” which is when near the end of the busy season one last heavy snowfall occurs which gives the city fresh snow and a bump of tourism before the season's over. But this year, Miracle March did not come.

Cardiovascular Perfusionist
Washington Park Denver, CO


A long time friend posed in her favorite park. As a frontline worker she explained to me how her hospital had been preparing for the incoming wave of coronavirus patients. At the time hospitals across the country had their eyes on Seattle and the Pacific Northwest, monitoring patterns, and leaning from the places that had been hit first and that were exiting their “peak.” An interesting thing she pointed out was that her roommates are more fearful than she is of contracting the virus.

Road Tripper
Rimrock Drive, CO


Pulling off to the side to an overlook, I met another fella admiring the red rock sunset. We begin to talk and he explains to me the history of the Santa Fe Trail. Uprooting your family and life, risking it all to to start anew on unknown land. All while knowing the dangers and perils of trespassing on ancient Native American land. We both imagined the spiritual experience of coming across the places that we now call national parks on horseback.

Creature Coffee
Austin, TX


Paid a friend a visit after returning from the road. Their son, born in early march, a few weeks before SXSW was set to take place. Both particularly grateful to be able to spend all their time together. If it weren’t for the pandemic, he would’ve been running round town working events nonstop during SXSW. And she would’ve been off maternity leave and back in the office.

Native Hostel
Austin, TX


One of the owners of Native Hostel. Native has been a staple space in town, a boutique hostel, popular bar and lounge among locals, all while hosting large events and parties in its space every week. It all came to a halt mid-March. He immediately started calling and emailing people he knew around town, trying to figure out how the now empty space could contribute to the fight against COVID-19.

Masks for Docs
Austin, TX


Masks For Docs, is an organization comprised of tech, business, design, and non-profit communities, all with the sole purpose of providing PPE to frontline healthcare workers across the world. This is the chapter Leader for the city of Austin. His purpose? To connect local organizations and people who own 3D printers, volunteer drivers, and healthcare facilities in need of protective gear, specifically masks. The facilities put in a request for masks, those requests get sent to owners of 3D printers to be produced, they then get picked up and packaged inside Native Hostels event space, and finally drivers deliver to the healthcare facilities.

Soupmakesitbetter
Austin, TX


A friend and local rapper in Austin. His stage name, Soupmakesitbetter. Pictured here in his studio a month after having dropped his four track Valentines Day EP titled 4 Play. COVID-19 came and shutdown Austins biggest festival, and along with it took away all the gigs and performances that musicians depend on. Soup had in-person meetings scheduled with record labels across the nation, and was supposed to headline a few of Austin’s most popular music venues in town. But all were put on hold or cancelled due to the virus. In the meantime he has been working on new tracks and finding talent to sign to his own record label, Paid Better.
